Welcome to the wizarding world of Harry Potter

What is Harry Potter?

The Harry Potter series was written by J.K. Rowling, with the first book being released in 1997. Since then, the series has grown to a total of seven books. There are a plethora of mythological elements weaved into the series, from centaurs to trolls to dementors. The books have also morphed into a movie series, only broadening it's range of age appeal.

About the Author

J.K. Rowling is a British author and philanthropist, widely known for her authorship of the Harry Potter series. She grew up a bookworm and wanted to be a writer from an early age, and went on to study at Exeter University. Mythological Elements

Dementors

Dementors are among the foulest creatures that walk this earth. They infest the darkest, filthiest places, they glory in decay and despair, they drain peace, hope, and happiness out of the air around them... - Professor Lupin, Prizoner of Azkaban

Centaurs

Centaurs are magical creatures whose head, torso, and arms were human-like and joined to a horse's lower body. In the series, they were considered to be their own individual species and not half-breeds. They also were known for being naturally talented in archery, healing magic, divination and astronomy.
